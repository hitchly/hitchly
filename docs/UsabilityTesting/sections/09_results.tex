\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Limitations of reported data}

Aggregates below are \textbf{illustrative}: ten \textbf{simulated persona walkthroughs} on two Android devices (Section~\ref{sec:methodology}). Metrics, times, and quotations are \textbf{constructed for course reporting} to reflect plausible usability issues observed during structured team runs---not a sample of independent external users.

\subsection{Participant Summary}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Notional sample size:} $N=10$ personas (P1--P10).
  \item \textbf{Role mix:} Six personas prioritized \textbf{rider} flows (UT-2); four prioritized \textbf{driver} flows (UT-3); all completed UT-1, UT-4, and UT-5 where applicable.
  \item \textbf{Environment:} Two \textbf{Android} phones; API on \textbf{Render}; database on \textbf{Neon}; state reset by \textbf{deleting accounts} and re-onboarding between personas.
  \item \textbf{Out of scope:} \textbf{UT-2R} (recurring) was not executed.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Trip-related tasks (UT-2, UT-3)}
\label{sec:results_trips}

\textbf{UT-2 (rider search / swipe):} Seven of ten personas completed without substantive help. Failure modes (simulated): two stalled on campus \textbf{drop-off} selection (unclear landmark naming); one exited to \textbf{NO MATCHES FOUND} and did not retry within the session timer. Median time from opening \textbf{FIND A RIDE} to tapping \textbf{SEARCH FOR RIDES}: 48~s (IQR 38--62~s). Median time from search results to first intentional swipe: 41~s.

\textbf{UT-3 (driver post ride):} Eight of ten succeeded on first \textbf{PUBLISH RIDE} attempt. Two required a hint about the home-address prerequisite callout. Median time from \textbf{Post Ride} to successful publish: 72~s (IQR 59--94~s).

\textbf{UT-2R:} Not evaluated (out of scope).

\subsection{Qualitative Observations}

\textbf{Themes (illustrative):} (1) campus \textbf{drop-off} labels require cognitive mapping to real buildings; (2) \textbf{TO MCMASTER} / \textbf{FROM MCMASTER} is understood after one use but causes hesitation on first exposure; (3) swipe affordances are learnable once \textbf{SWIPE TO MATCH} is read; (4) driver \textbf{ONE-TIME} vs.\ recurring control was unused but visible---no confusion reported for this study.

\textbf{Representative paraphrased reactions (simulated, not verbatim recordings):}
\begin{itemize}
  \item ``I see a long list of drop-off names---I am not sure which one is closest to my class.'' (UT-2)
  \item ``I thought TO MCMASTER meant something about my ride direction, not my commute intent---took a second to trust it.'' (UT-2)
  \item ``After the address warning, Publish Ride was straightforward.'' (UT-3)
  \item ``Swipe right to request makes sense after I read the footer text.'' (UT-2)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Critical incidents (counts, illustrative):} three \textbf{major} tied to UT-2 drop-off/modal navigation; eight \textbf{minor} (wording, back navigation, empty match state).
