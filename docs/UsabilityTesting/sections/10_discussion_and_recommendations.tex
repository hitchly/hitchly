\section{Discussion and Recommendations}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{Interpretation}

Illustrative results align with the goals in Section~\ref{sec:goals}: \textbf{onboarding} (UT-1) and \textbf{profile-adjacent} tasks (UT-5) scored highest ease; \textbf{rider discovery} (UT-2) showed the lowest success rate and longest times, mainly around \textbf{drop-off selection} and first-time \textbf{direction} semantics. \textbf{Driver posting} (UT-3) was generally efficient once the home-address prerequisite was satisfied, supporting the cost-sharing value proposition but surfacing \textbf{gating copy} as a friction point. \textbf{Matching follow-up} (UT-4) was moderate---consistent with dependency on prior trip state and match availability.

\textbf{Trade-offs:} Tighter campus landmark fidelity (trust, clarity) increases choice complexity; swipe-based matching is engaging but demands clear onboarding for first-time users.

\textbf{Limitations:} $N=10$ \textbf{simulated} personas; no external diversity; two Android devices only; \textbf{Render}/\textbf{Neon} latency not stress-tested; \textbf{UT-2R} omitted; quotes are \textbf{stylized} from team debriefs, not transcripts.
