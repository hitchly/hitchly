\section{Goals of Usability Testing}
\label{sec:goals}

The following goals guide session design and success criteria. They should be measurable using the metrics in Section~\ref{sec:metrics}.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Onboarding and trust:} Assess whether participants can complete registration, McMaster-affiliated verification, and role selection (rider/driver) without undue confusion or abandonment.
  \item \textbf{Core rider journey:} Evaluate discoverability and completion of finding or requesting a ride, understanding trip details, and confirming participation within the app.
  \item \textbf{Core driver journey:} Evaluate offering a ride, setting or understanding route and schedule-related inputs, and managing passenger-related actions as implemented.
  \item \textbf{Matching and trip state:} Observe comprehension of match suggestions, trip status, and navigation between related screens.
  \item \textbf{Error recovery:} Record where users deviate, hesitate, or commit errors, and whether they recover without moderator intervention (within a reasonable threshold).
  \item \textbf{Subjective satisfaction:} Quantify perceived ease of use and confidence via standardized scales (e.g., SUS) and structured debrief responses.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent Team members may refine goal wording after piloting the script with one internal participant to ensure tasks remain feasible on the build under test.
